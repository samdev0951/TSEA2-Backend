# The GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)

The Graphics Processing Unit (or Graphics Card) is like the visual part of your brain. It is responsible for the complex mathematical processing that delivers pixels to your screen that is recognisable to the human eye. The Graphics Card holds information its own VRAM (similar to the RAM), which is useful for rapidly processing complex 3D shapes and shaders - which is why these are recommended for gamers! Many CPUs come with their own integrated graphics processing, which is typically a lot slower than a modern Graphics Card as it is packed into a tiny chip without its own dedicated cooling.
